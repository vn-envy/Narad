The Big Idea - What Are We Actually Building?

A very good personal assistant who wakes up before you, reads every job board, figures out which ones match you, writes a custom resume, fills the application, and remembers every outcome.

Scout

Reads every job board every morning across 50+ sources

Evaluate

Figures out if you fit using AI reasoning, not keyword counting

Craft

Writes a custom resume and cover letter for each role

Apply

Fills forms, shows preview, submits only when you nod

Track

Remembers everything and learns patterns

First Principle #1 - SCOUT

The Finding Hand

Like a metal detector at the beach. You walk the whole beach, and it beeps when there is something good underground.

50+ Sources Daily

Amazon, Google, Microsoft, LinkedIn, Greenhouse, Lever, Ashby, Workday, and more

Deduplication

Same job on multiple sources counts once. Clean pipeline.

Profile Scoring

Every job scored against your target roles, skills, locations

Custom Access

Microsoft PCSX API, Amazon board, Google Careers, LinkedIn guest API

Most systems check 1-2 sources. We check 50+. That is the whole beach vs one spot.

First Principle #2 - EVALUATE

The Choosing Brain

Like a food critic reading a menu. They do not just count "spicy" -- they reason about whether you will like the dish.

10 Scoring Dimensions

CV Match

Does your experience fit the role?

Level Strategy

Step up, sideways, or down?

Comp Fit

Does the salary range work?

Skills Gap

What are you missing?

Story Readiness

Can you tell a good interview story?

Location Fit

Remote? Willing to relocate?

Culture Signals

How does the JD say they work?

Growth Potential

Will this role stretch you?

Timing

Actively hiring or building pipeline?

Network Access

Do you know anyone there?

Keyword counting gives you 3/10. AI reasoning gives you 9/10. We do the latter.

First Principle #3 - CRAFT

The Writing Hand

Like a bespoke tailor. You bring your measurements, the tailor cuts the fabric just for you -- every single time.

Master Resume + JD

Merges your full history with the job description

Summary Rewrite

Professional summary rewritten for each role

Relevant Bullets

Most relevant experience selected automatically

Natural Keywords

Keywords inserted naturally, not stuffed

ATS-Safe Output

DOCX and PDF that pass resume scanners

Cover Letter

Tailored letter for each company/role

Same resume for every job = same outfit for a wedding AND a hike. Both wrong differently.

First Principle #4 - APPLY

The Careful Hand

Like filling a DMV form. Your assistant fills it, shows it to you, and only submits when you nod.

Portal Detection

Recognizes Greenhouse vs Lever vs Workday from the URL

Invisible Browser

Playwright fills every field, uploads resume, answers screening questions

Preview Screenshot

Full screenshot of the filled form for your review

Human Approval

Only submits when you say yes. Never on its own.

The 3 Hard Guardrails

❌Never auto-submit without human approval
❌Never invent experience you do not have
❌Never leave the official application page
Fast enough to save hours. Careful enough to never embarrass you.

First Principle #5 - TRACK

The Remembering Hand

Like a gardener journal. Plant seeds (apply), note which sprout (interviews), which flower (offers), which do not (rejections). Next season, plant more of what worked.

Full Pipeline

discovered - evaluated - tailored - applied - interviewing - offer - rejected

Follow-up Timing

Reminds you to follow up in 7 days if no response

Liveness Checker

Verifies if old listings still accept applications

Pattern Analysis

You score highest on TPM roles at big tech. Double down.

No tracking = guessing. Tracking = learning. Every rejection is data for the next win.

No Cloud Deployment

That is a feature, not a limitation.
